1 Research Background and Objectives

Barcelona, as one of the most representative Mediterranean cities in Europe, provides rich case material for urban planning research due to its unique urban form and spatial structure. Since the construction of the Eixample district planned by Ildefons Cerdà in the 19th century, Barcelona's urban development model has attracted much attention.

Research Objectives

The core objective of this study is to generate comparable urban spatial images by controlling variables such as perspective, scale, and lighting, to reveal the impact of different development strategies on urban morphology, and to provide visual reference bases for planning decisions.

This study sets up the following main scenarios for comparative analysis:

2 Research Methodology

2.1 Scenario Design Framework

This study adopts the Scenario Planning method to explore the impact of different decision choices on urban spatial morphology by constructing multiple possible future development paths.

  • Internal Consistency: Logical relationships remain coherent.
  • Contrast Significance: Sufficient differences exist across scenarios.
  • Realistic Possibility: Scenarios are based on existing development models.
  • Planning Relevance: Scenarios relate closely to contemporary practice.

Main Research Findings
  1. Density is decisive: density changes reshape spatial structure.

  3. Policy orientation affects quality: environmental and cultural value shift materially.
  4. Mixed strategies are more resilient: moderate combinations often perform better.

8 Research Limitations and Outlook

8.1 Research Limitations

  • Idealization of image generation: generated images may be overly idealized.
  • Limitation of static perspectives: static views cannot show all temporal changes.
  • Lack of quantitative analysis: this sample focuses on qualitative visual analysis.
  • Simplification of socio-economic factors: scenario design omits some real-world complexity.

8.2 Future Research Directions

  • Construction of multi-dimensional evaluation systems.
  • Dynamic evolution simulation using CA or ABM.
  • Public participation mechanisms via visual planning tools.
  • Empirical verification research on typical case cities.

Through systematic scenario design and visual presentation, this study provides a direct reference for understanding the spatial consequences of urban development decisions.
